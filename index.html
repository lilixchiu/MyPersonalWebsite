Valorant Guide for Beginners

What is Valorant?

Riot Games's first ever FPS (first person shooter) game, Valorant, sister to Riot's most succesful franchise 'League of Legends', was released in 2020. It is described as a 5v5 character-based tactical FPS where precise gunplay meets unique agent abilities. Two teams, defenders vs attackers, play to win 13 rounds on various maps. Valorant is the perfect blend between CSGO and Overwatch, blending exciting agent abilities with traditional gun-play to bring in new dimensional gameplays.There's much to learn, but fret not! Valorant is a fairly simple and user friendly FPS game that is quick to pick up. This guide will cover all the basics you need to know to get started!
Get Started

In a tatical 5v5 shooter game, each player will select an agent at the start of the round. You will either start on the side of the 'attackers' where you will push onto a site and plant the 'spike' (bomb) and prevent the 'defenders' from defusing it or killing everyone in your team. The attackers win by either letting the bomb explode or killing everyone in the defender's team. The defenders' objective is to diffuse the bomb if it has been planted or to kill everyone in the attacker team. First team to win 13 rounds wins the entire game. Each round, you only have 1 life and 100 health. There will be exceptions to these due to agent and shield variables (We will get into the details later).
